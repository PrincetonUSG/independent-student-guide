INDEPENDENT DRAW OPTIONS

The University defines an independent student as one who holds no meal contract whatsoever with a University dining facility or with an off-campus club.

The Spelman Hall, 2 Dickinson Street Co-op, Brown Co-op, and Independent Draws are offered to provide independent students appropriate cooking facilities. Applications for these draws are closed for the '04-'05 academic year.

There is no Wait List for these draws and no Room Improvement process. If you apply but do not select/draw a room in any of these draws and wish to participate in Regular Draw, you must submit the Upperclass and Residential College Draw Application form at the appropriate time (weighted rating points for these draws do not carry over to Regular Upperclass Draw).

Groups of eight or fewer may enter the Independent Draw, but at least one half of the members of any group must be independent.

Spelman Draw

Groups of four, eight, or twelve students from the classes of 2005 and 2006 may enter the Spelman Draw. This draw is closed for the '04-'05 academic year.

The following scale determines your group's weighting for the Spelman Draw:

2 Dickinson Street Co-op Draw

This is a self-governing co-op, and has historically served vegetarian meals. If you select a room in this draw you must sign the Pledge of Independence and you must be a member of the cooperative. For more information about the 2 Dickinson Street Co-op, please call Indrany Datta-Barua, x6-9611.

The order of the draw will be determined by the weighted rating (average) of each application. The weighted ratings are:

Class of 2005 Independent3 points
Class of 2006 Independent2 points

Brown Co-op Draw

This co-op was initiated in response to student request for a cooperative dining arrangement that includes meat as part of the general diet. For more information on the Brown Co-op, please contact Brian Skerratt, x6-8205

The Brown Co-op is set up in the kitchen on the second floor of Brown Hall. A group of rooms totaling 18 beds has been reserved for students who wish to become members of this co-op. These rooms are:

Singles:213, 214, 215, 217, 222
Double:210
Triple:211
Quads:212, 219

Any student or group of students (eight or less) who wish to participate in the Brown Co-op Draw must complete the Special Preliminary Draws Application form and return it to the Housing Office by the appropriate time.

If you select a room in this draw you must sign the Pledge of Independence and participate in the Brown Co-op. Students joining the Brown Co-op pay a $50.00 fee for holding a locked kitchen key.

The order of the draw will be determined by the weighted rating (average) of each application. The weighted ratings are:
